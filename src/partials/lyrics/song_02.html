The Old Man

He reaches for the harness one more time, leather like the hands that hold the reigns. She flicks at imaginary flies, Waits for him to get them underway.

She sees his breath, the words are sweet and low, a cluck and quiet whisper just for her, The runners nearly silent on the snow A dusting on a night so crisp and clear.
